TRIO & GEAR UP: Annual Performance Report Data Submission
Window set annually by ED. Typically opens January through February. Verify with your program officer each year.
Verify before acting. APR portal access and submission timelines have been subject to administrative changes since early 2025. Confirm your current window and submission requirements directly with your program officer and via trio.ed.gov before beginning data entry.
Why "program officer sets the window": APR submission deadlines vary by TRIO program type and are issued annually through program-specific Dear Project Director letters. Talent Search submits aggregated data on a different schedule than SSS or Upward Bound, which submit participant-level records. There is no single universal date that applies to all programs. Your program officer and your annual APR notice are the authoritative source for your specific deadline each year.

All TRIO grantees are required to submit Annual Performance Report data through ED's TRIO APR Web Portal at trio.ed.gov. The APR is the primary federal accountability mechanism for TRIO programs. It captures participant counts, eligibility documentation, service delivery data, and outcome measures against the program's approved objectives. Inaccurate or incomplete APR submissions are among the most common findings in TRIO monitoring visits. Data that cannot be traced back to participant files at the time of submission is not defensible.

What to have ready before your APR window opens
  • Participant eligibility documentation: first-generation, low-income, or disability status verified and on file for every participant counted
  • Service delivery logs: each service type reported must be traceable to a dated record
  • Outcome data: secondary school completion, postsecondary enrollment, and financial aid application rates reconciled against participant files
  • Prior-year APR comparison: significant drops in any metric require a narrative explanation
  • Authorized organizational representative designation current in the TRIO APR Web Portal
Source: Higher Education Act Title IV, Part A, Subpart 2 · ED TRIO Programs, ed.gov · TRIO APR Web Portal: trio.ed.gov · Deadline confirmed annually in your program's Dear Project Director letter
Internal Action
Mid-Year Grant Expenditure Pace Review
Recommended: February through March. Not a federal filing date.

Federal grant budgets are subject to the Uniform Guidance requirement that costs be necessary, reasonable, and allocable to the grant (2 CFR §200.403). Programs that underspend significantly by mid-year risk end-of-year scrambles to obligate funds before the budget period closes, which often produces undocumented or poorly justified expenditures. A pace problem identified in February can be corrected. One identified in August rarely can.

What to review internally
  • Pull current expenditure report against total grant budget by object code
  • Identify delayed hires, unfilled positions, or postponed purchases
  • Project forward to budget period end: will any line item be significantly under or over?
  • Document a corrective plan for any line item requiring reallocation
  • Review your Notice of Grant Award for any budget modification thresholds specific to your award. Under ED's expanded authorities, grantees have significant latitude to make budget transfers without prior approval — confirm the applicable threshold with your program officer before any significant reallocation. See 2 CFR §200.308 for the general framework.
Source: 2 CFR §200.403 (Uniform Guidance, factors affecting allowability of costs) · Uniform Guidance, ecfr.gov · Budget revision framework: 2 CFR §200.308 · Budget modification thresholds: review your specific Notice of Grant Award and confirm with your program officer

Data Privacy Agreement (DPA) Renewal Audit
Recommended: January. Not a regulatory deadline — a standing governance obligation.

Access programs use a range of third-party platforms for case management, advising, participant tracking, and communication. Every platform that touches participant data is subject to FERPA's data governance requirements (20 U.S.C. §1232g) and, for platforms used with students under 13, COPPA (15 U.S.C. §6501). DPAs expire. Vendors change terms of service. A DPA that was signed two years ago may no longer reflect the vendor's current data practices. January is when to audit the full DPA inventory before a new program year of tool adoption begins.

What to audit in January
  • Complete inventory of all platforms in use: approved and unapproved, institutional and individual
  • Confirm a current, signed DPA exists for every platform that accesses participant data
  • Review expiration dates: any DPA expiring before September 30 requires renewal action now
  • Check vendor terms of service for changes since the DPA was signed
  • Flag any platform adopted by staff since the last audit that does not yet have a DPA
Federal basis: FERPA, studentprivacy.ed.gov · 20 U.S.C. §1232g · COPPA: 15 U.S.C. §6501 · No single federal DPA renewal date — this is a standing obligation triggered by contract terms and vendor changes

TRIO Continuation Application or Non-Competing Continuation
Typically April through May for programs in years 2–5 of a project period. Verify with your program officer annually.
Verify before acting. Continuation procedures and timelines have been subject to administrative changes across TRIO program types since early 2025. Do not assume prior-year procedures apply. Confirm your current continuation requirements directly with your program officer before preparing materials.
Why "program officer sets the window": TRIO continuation applications are not on a uniform federal calendar. ED issues continuation notices on a program-by-program basis. Your program officer sets the submission window. The federal obligation — continued eligibility documentation and performance reporting — is uniform. The deadline is not.

Programs in years 2 through 5 of a TRIO project period submit a non-competing continuation application (or equivalent) to receive their next-year funding installment. This submission requires updated budget justification, confirmation of continued eligibility, and in some cycles, a narrative response to prior-year APR findings. Programs with unresolved APR findings from the prior year should expect additional scrutiny in the continuation review.

What to have ready
  • Updated budget narrative: any changes from the approved budget require justification
  • Confirmation of grantee organization's continued eligibility status
  • Response narrative for any prior-year APR findings or conditions
  • Current authorized organizational representative on file with ED
  • Any required assurances or certifications current in Grants.gov/SAM.gov
Confirm with your program officer. ED TRIO Programs, trio.ed.gov · Continuation requirements vary by program type and project year

Internal Action
FERPA Annual Notification: Review and Distribute
Recommended: May through June. No single federal filing date.

34 CFR §99.7 requires programs that maintain education records to notify participants of their FERPA rights annually. For access programs working with minor participants, this includes notifying parents or guardians. The notification must be provided by any means reasonably likely to reach families and must be available in required home languages. End-of-year review ensures the notification is current and distribution is documented before the next program year begins.

What to review and document
  • Notification content current: rights to inspect, amend, and consent to disclosure
  • Addresses participants' rights for records maintained by the access program specifically
  • Distribution method documented: how was it sent, to whom, and when
  • Available in all required home languages for your participant population
  • Opt-out procedure for directory information documented and accessible
Source: FERPA, studentprivacy.ed.gov · 34 CFR §99.7 · Annual standing obligation; no single federal filing date
Internal Action
AI Tool Inventory: Mid-Year Vetting Review
Recommended: May through June. Not a regulatory deadline.

New AI tools are adopted by access program staff continuously throughout the year. Many are adopted without formal vetting, DPA review, or governing board documentation. A mid-year inventory review surfaces tools that have entered the program's workflow since January without completing the approval process. Tools used for advising, outreach, or case management that touch participant data without a signed DPA represent a FERPA exposure that grows with every additional interaction.

What to review mid-year
  • Ask every staff member to list AI tools currently in use for program work — approved and unapproved
  • Flag any tool not on the approved inventory from January
  • Confirm DPA status for every newly identified tool before continued use
  • Review any tool used for participant-facing communication or advising for age-appropriate content safeguards
  • Document the review: date, reviewer, tools identified, actions taken
Federal basis: FERPA, studentprivacy.ed.gov · 20 U.S.C. §1232g · COPPA: 15 U.S.C. §6501 · AI governance review is recommended practice grounded in existing federal data obligations
Q3: July through September
Hard Deadline
Federal Grant Budget Period Close
September 30 — federal fiscal year close. All obligations must be incurred by this date.

Under the Uniform Guidance (2 CFR §200.344), all costs charged to a federal grant must be incurred within the approved budget period. For grants aligned to the federal fiscal year, that period closes September 30. Costs incurred after September 30 cannot be charged to the prior budget period. Final expenditure reports and financial close-out processes follow the budget period end. Programs that have not met expenditure targets by this date have exhausted their window for corrective action.

What to complete before September 30
  • All purchase orders issued and goods or services received
  • All personnel costs incurred and documented through time-and-effort records
  • Final expenditure report drafted and reviewed against budget
  • Any unexpended funds flagged for program officer discussion — do not carry over without authorization
  • All participant records for the program year finalized and filed
Source: 2 CFR §200.344 (Uniform Guidance, closeout) · Uniform Guidance, ecfr.gov · Budget period end date confirmed in your specific grant award notice
Internal Action
Time-and-Effort Records: Year-End Reconciliation
Recommended: August through September, before budget period close.

The Uniform Guidance (2 CFR §200.430) requires that salaries charged to federal grants be supported by records that reflect the actual activity of each employee. For access programs, this means time-and-effort documentation for every staff member whose salary is charged entirely or partially to the grant. Federal monitoring visits consistently identify time-and-effort records as a primary documentation gap. Year-end reconciliation ensures that personnel cost documentation matches what was reported in the APR and what will appear in the final expenditure report.

What to reconcile before September 30
  • Time-and-effort records complete for every grant-funded employee for the full program year
  • Records reflect actual activity, not budget estimates — after-the-fact certifications are a finding
  • Semi-annual certifications (for employees working solely on one federal program) signed and on file
  • Personnel activity reports (for employees splitting time across multiple grants or activities) complete and reconciled
  • Any staff changes mid-year documented with updated position descriptions and effort allocations
Source: 2 CFR §200.430 (compensation for personal services) · Uniform Guidance §200.430, ecfr.gov
Internal Action
AI Incident Response Protocol: Annual Review
Recommended: August through September. Not a regulatory deadline.

Access programs using AI tools in advising, outreach, or case management face a growing category of AI-related incidents: data exposures through non-approved tools, harmful output reaching program participants, vendor failures affecting program data, and staff use of AI in violation of program policy. An incident response protocol that has not been reviewed in the past twelve months may not reflect the current tool landscape, the current staff roster, or current guidance from ED on participant data protection. Year-end is when to review and update before the next program year begins.

What to review and update
  • Notification chain current: names, roles, and contact methods for each step
  • Protocol covers all five incident types: data exposure, harmful output, deepfake or misuse, vendor failure, policy violation
  • 24-hour documentation requirements named: what gets written down, by whom, and where it is filed
  • Communication review step documented: who approves communications to participants or families before they go out
  • Protocol signed and dated by program director and filed with the organization's compliance records
Federal basis: FERPA, studentprivacy.ed.gov · 20 U.S.C. §1232g · Incident response review is recommended practice grounded in FERPA breach notification obligations and grant assurance requirements

TRIO Recompetition: Application Preparation Window
Applications typically due April through May. Preparation window: October through December of the prior year.
Why "program officer sets the window": TRIO competitive grant cycles are announced by ED through the Federal Register and Grants.gov. The announcement date and application deadline vary by program type and fiscal year. The preparation window listed here is recommended practice for programs in year 4 or 5 of a project period who will recompete in the next cycle.
Current status as of March 2026: The Talent Search competition (ED-GRANT-26-022) was announced March 17, 2026 with a May 1, 2026 deadline. Other TRIO program competitions have been disrupted — as of early 2026, ED had not announced competitions for several TRIO programs despite programs reaching the end of their project periods. Monitor Grants.gov, the Federal Register, and the Council for Opportunity in Education (coenet.org) for announcements specific to your program type.

TRIO grants are awarded competitively on five-year project periods. Programs in year 4 or 5 should begin preparing recompetition materials in Q4 of the year prior to the application deadline. The selection criteria for TRIO competitive applications include the quality of the project design, the grantee's past performance, the quality of personnel, and the adequacy of resources. Past performance is scored using APR data. Programs with strong, documented performance histories score higher. That documentation is built across the life of the grant, not assembled in the weeks before submission.

What to prepare in Q4
  • Pull five-year APR performance data: participant outcomes, service delivery rates, and trend analysis
  • Identify narrative gaps: where performance met or exceeded targets and where it did not, with explanation
  • Update needs assessment documentation for the target population
  • Review current project description against what the program actually delivers — alignment gaps are a finding
  • Confirm organizational eligibility for the next project period
  • Monitor Grants.gov and the Federal Register for the new competition announcement
Monitor for announcements: Grants.gov · ED TRIO Programs, trio.ed.gov · Selection criteria: current program notice, published with each competition
Internal Action
Annual AI and Data Governance Review: Access Program Edition
Recommended: October through November. Not a regulatory deadline — grounded in standing federal data obligations.

No single federal regulation requires an annual AI governance review for TRIO or GEAR UP programs. This review is recommended practice built on obligations that do apply: FERPA governs every tool that accesses participant records (20 U.S.C. §1232g); the Uniform Guidance requires that grantees maintain adequate internal controls as a condition of award (2 CFR §200.303); and grant assurances obligate programs to demonstrate responsible data governance throughout the budget period. As AI tool adoption in access programs has accelerated, the gap between what programs actually use and what they can document has widened significantly. A Q4 review closes that gap before a new program year opens and before any recompetition documentation is assembled — both contexts where undocumented tool use becomes a liability.

What the annual review covers
  • Complete AI and third-party platform inventory: every tool used for advising, case management, outreach, or participant communication — approved and unapproved, institutional and individual staff tools
  • DPA status for every tool that accesses participant data: confirmed, expiring, or missing — any missing DPA requires resolution before continued use
  • Uniform Guidance internal controls check (2 CFR §200.303): do current tool-use practices meet the grantee's obligation to safeguard federal program assets and participant information?
  • Incident response protocol: current, signed, and filed with program compliance records
  • Staff AI governance training: documented for all program staff who use AI tools in program work — attendance, content, facilitator, date
  • Output review policy documented for every participant-facing AI tool in use
  • Full documentation producible within one business day if requested by a federal reviewer
Federal basis: FERPA, studentprivacy.ed.gov · 20 U.S.C. §1232g · Uniform Guidance internal controls: 2 CFR §200.303 · ecfr.gov §200.303 · Grant assurance requirements: your program's Notice of Grant Award · TLF Access Programs resources: languagefirm.org/theaccessprograms

Staff Training Documentation: Year-End Compliance Record
Recommended: November through December. Not a single federal filing date.

Federal monitoring visits for TRIO and GEAR UP programs routinely examine staff training records as evidence of program capacity and responsible administration. Reviewers ask whether staff have received documented training on FERPA obligations, grant compliance requirements, and — increasingly — AI governance and participant data protection. Training that occurred but was not documented did not happen from a compliance standpoint. Year-end is when to confirm that all required training is documented, signed, and filed before the next program year begins.

What to document before December 31
  • FERPA training: attendance log, content summary, facilitator name, date — for all program staff
  • AI governance training: signed completion records for all staff using AI tools in program work
  • Grant compliance orientation: documented for any staff hired mid-year
  • Incident response training: confirmation that all staff know the notification chain and their role in it
  • Training records filed in a central, accessible location — not in individual staff files
Federal basis: Grant assurance requirements under the program's Notice of Grant Award · FERPA, studentprivacy.ed.gov · TLF Staff Coverage Package: languagefirm.org/theaccessprograms
